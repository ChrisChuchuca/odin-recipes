Chankonabe

Description

Chankonabe (ちゃんこ鍋) is a Japanese stew (a type of nabemono or one-pot dish) commonly eaten in vast quantity by sumo wrestlers as part of a weight-gain diet.

Ingredients

  • Tsukune
  • Pork belly - thinly sliced
  • Chicken thigh fillets - cut to large bite-size pieces
  • Chineses cabbage
  • Bok choy
  • Carrot - thinly sliced into rectangles
  • Shallot/scallions - cut 2" long
  • Shiitake mushrooms - stem removed
  • Enoki Mushrooms - bottom of stem removed
  • Kamaboko - thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Place all the stew ingredients—the meatballs, seafood, pork belly, chicken, tofu, carrot slices, napa cabbage, negi, green onions, and mushrooms—on platters. Set the platters on the table.
  2. Keep the broth at a simmer the entire time. If the liquid gets low, add a little water or chicken broth to have enough liquid to heat the noodles or rice at the end.
  3. When diners are ready for the final course, remove any solids in the broth and add the rice or precooked udon noodles. Simmer until heated through, then ladle into the soup bowls and serve.
